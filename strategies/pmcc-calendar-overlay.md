# Poor Man's Covered Call + Calendar Overlay

## Structure

- Initiate approximately **10 calendar days before the company's scheduled earnings announcement**.
- Buy one deep ITM LEAPS call (typically Delta 0.80–0.95) with 9–24 months until expiration to serve as a synthetic long stock position.
- Sell one shorter-dated OTM call to generate premium from elevated pre-earnings implied volatility.
- Buy one call at the same strike as the short call with an expiration approximately one week or one month later, creating a calendar overlay that spans the earnings event.
- The calendar overlay is intended to improve management flexibility around earnings while preserving the capital efficiency of a standard PMCC.

---

## Defined-Risk Profile

- Maximum loss is limited to the net debit paid for the LEAPS and calendar overlay, partially offset by premiums collected from the short call.
- Risk is predefined and substantially lower than purchasing 100 shares of stock.
- Capital efficiency is a primary objective of the strategy.

---

## Directional Fit

- Moderately Bullish
- Bullish
- Long-term positive outlook

Best suited for stocks expected to appreciate following earnings while benefiting from elevated pre-earnings option premiums.

---

## Volatility Exposure

- Positive Delta from the LEAPS.
- Positive Vega from the LEAPS.
- Negative Theta from the LEAPS.
- Positive Theta from the short call.
- The calendar overlay partially offsets the impact of implied volatility changes and provides additional flexibility around earnings.
- Strategy is designed to take advantage of elevated implied volatility before earnings while managing the subsequent volatility crush.

---

## Entry Criteria

- Enter approximately **10 calendar days before earnings**.
- Bullish outlook on the underlying stock.
- Elevated implied volatility provides attractive premium on the short call.
- Deep ITM LEAPS available with sufficient liquidity.
- Tight bid/ask spreads across all option legs.
- Calendar overlay selected to extend beyond the earnings announcement when appropriate.

---

## Exit Plan

Exit the strategy when one or more of the following occur:

- Profit objective has been achieved.
- The post-earnings opportunity has concluded.
- The bullish thesis is no longer valid.
- Remaining reward no longer justifies the capital at risk.
- The LEAPS no longer provides an efficient synthetic stock replacement.

---

## Management Rules

- Monitor the short call as earnings approaches.
- Decide whether to hold, roll, or close the short call before expiration based on remaining premium and assignment risk.
- Use the calendar overlay to improve flexibility around the earnings event.
- Reassess the entire position immediately following the post-earnings implied volatility crush.
- Roll or replace the LEAPS when its remaining time to expiration is no longer optimal.
- Continuously evaluate capital efficiency after each adjustment.

---

## Not This Strategy When

- Bearish or neutral outlook.
- Limited expected upside following earnings.
- Poor option liquidity.
- Wide bid/ask spreads.
- LEAPS are excessively expensive due to unusually high implied volatility.
- A simpler defined-risk strategy provides a superior risk-adjusted opportunity.

---

## Primary Risks

- Time decay on the LEAPS.
- Changes in implied volatility.
- Post-earnings volatility crush.
- Early assignment of the short call.
- Large adverse price movement.
- Increased management complexity due to multiple option legs.
- Execution risk when rolling positions around earnings.

---

## Evaluation Notes

Evaluate this strategy using:

- Capital required.
- Effective leverage versus purchasing 100 shares.
- Return on capital.
- Maximum defined risk.
- Premium captured before earnings.
- Impact of post-earnings implied volatility crush.
- Effectiveness of the calendar overlay in managing the short call through earnings.
- Liquidity of all option legs.
- Expected management complexity.
